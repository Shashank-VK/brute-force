Participants must design and develop a comprehensive Club Hub Web Application for their college
technical club. This platform goes beyond a typical competitive programming site - it serves as the official
digital identity of the club, combining member management, event hosting, challenge systems, project
showcases, and a rich media gallery into one cohesive, modern web experience.
Inspired by platforms such as LeetCode, HackerRank, and Devfolio, the application must incorporate
club-centric features that make it the go-to hub for every member from curious freshers to experienced
seniors.

College technical clubs often lack a centralised, professional web presence. Activities are scattered
across WhatsApp groups, Google Forms, and social media pages, making it difficult for students to
discover events, track their growth, or understand what the club has achieved. Participants must solve the
following key pain points:
No single source of truth

Club info is fragmented - members cannot find team details, past
projects, or results in one place.

Invisible team members

Club members and their roles have no public visibility on any dedicated
platform.

Poor event discovery

Students miss events due to lack of an organised calendar and
notification system.

No challenge ecosystem

There is no structured challenge system with real-time submission
tracking and leaderboards.

Project graveyard

Club projects are rarely documented or showcased, reducing their impact
and learning value.

Gallery chaos

Event photos and memories are buried in drives or group chats with no
curated public archive.
